Some notes

Using two layers may take upto a few minutes depending on how popular or old the article is. To identify main branches of research originating from the primary article, I have used the name of the last author as a filter criteria as it reliably identifies individual research groups. Multiple citations by a particular group is indicative of a distinct line of research linked to the primary article. The minimum number of citations by a group to qualify as a separate branch can be set in the 'Threshold' value box. A higher number would represent 'thicker' branches and also lead to fewer keywords which are present only in these branches. The default is 1. The example link generated at random may on some occasions lead to non-existent papers.
